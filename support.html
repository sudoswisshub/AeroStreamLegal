Frequently Asked Questions

How does AeroStream work?

AeroStream uses real-time flight data from the OpenSky Network to show you aircraft currently in the air around your location. The app displays live positions, altitudes, speeds, and flight paths on an interactive map.

Why does AeroStream need location access?

Location access allows AeroStream to center the map on your current position and show you flights in your immediate vicinity. This feature is optional but recommended for the best experience.

Is my data private?

Yes! AeroStream does not collect, store, or share your personal data. Location data is used only temporarily to fetch nearby flights and is never transmitted to our servers. Please see our Privacy Policy for more details.

What data source does AeroStream use?

AeroStream uses the OpenSky Network API, a free and open-source community-driven network that provides real-time air traffic data from ADS-B receivers around the world.

Why aren't I seeing any flights?

There could be several reasons:

  • No aircraft are currently flying in your visible area
  • Your location may be in a remote area with less air traffic
  • Try zooming out or panning the map to a different location
  • Check your internet connection

Can I use AeroStream without location access?

Yes! If you skip location access, the app will default to showing flights around a preset location (Zurich, Switzerland). You can still pan the map to any location worldwide and view flights in that area.
